Our Services

Concept and Process Planning with Lean Assessment

We begin with a process overview and material flow analysis, calculating cycle times based on production targets. Our team assesses joining technologies, robot utilisation, and 2D layouts for feasibility and space optimisation. With a strong focus on lean assessment, process validation, and productivity enhancement, we help BIW assembly lines maximise efficiency and safety.

Robotic Cell Design and Feasibility

Our services include building robotic cells in a 3D environment, evaluating panel orientation feasibility, and defining material flow. We also verify metal joining and assembly procedures and develop equipment pre-BOMs, ensuring robot reachability.

Detailed Robotic Simulation

We create detailed work cell layouts with a strong focus on operator safety and ergonomics. Tooling validation is a key part of this phase, as is the avoidance of joint locks or singularities. We conduct comprehensive risk and safety assessments, designing work cells that are easy to maintain. Simulation ensures that robots can perform all desired tasks efficiently and that cycle times are optimised.

Offline Robot Programming

This includes creating collision-free paths, calculating and optimising cycle times, and ensuring robots are interlocked properly. We prepare detailed sequences of operations and use Real-Time Controllers (RCS) to verify robot behaviour, addressing any jerky motion or unnecessary waiting. This ensures all robots and equipment work in synchronisation while adhering to interlocks and I/O signals within the required cycle time.

Simulation Cells Calibration Using POD Data/Robot Backups

We ensure precise cell alignment using POD data or robot backups while reconstructing objects based on point cloud scanned data for enhanced accuracy and efficiency.

Event-Based Simulation and Virtual Commissioning (VC)

We build Virtual Commissioning environments using Process Simulate, utilising CEE facilities. We apply sensor conditions, build logic blocks, and monitor signals within the simulation environment. This enables the validation of robot logic conditions, including all required I/O instructions, to ensure that the system behaves as intended in a real-world scenario.
