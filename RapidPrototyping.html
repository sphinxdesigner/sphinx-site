Technologies We Work On Polymer

Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)

Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) is one of the most widely used manufacturing techniques for low-volume production of nylon material parts. The process fuses the powder raw material using a laser. Materials available for this process are similar to injection molded polyamides with glass-filled types and polystyrene. SLS is a relatively new technology that has so far been used for rapid prototyping. Its most common application is in prototype parts early in the design cycle, such as investment casting patterns, automotive hardware, and end-use parts for aerospace, military, etc.

Stereolithography (SLA)

Stereolithography (SLA) is the earliest developed 3D Printing technique. The process utilizes a laser beam to cure a photopolymer liquid resin layer by layer. Materials for SLA are similar to those available for the Injection Molding process. Properties achieved are similar to ABS, Polypropylene (PP), Transparent Polycarbonate (PC), Food grade, Medical grade, and Flame-retardant grade materials. With large industrial-size SLA machine build platforms, the overall 3D Printing time required for the majority of projects ranges from 1 to 3 days.

Multi Jet Fusion (MJF)

Multi Jet Fusion (MJF) uses an inkjet array to selectively apply fusing and detailing agents across a bed of nylon powder, which are then fused by heating elements into a solid layer. After each layer, powder is distributed on top of the bed and the process repeats until the part is complete.

PolyJet Printing

PolyJet printing is a rapid prototyping process that uses additive manufacturing. The printers have two or more jetting heads (one set for the model and one set for the support material) that spray outlines of the part, layer by layer.

Digital Light Processing (DLP)

Digital Light Processing (DLP) is similar to Stereolithography. It uses a more conventional light source, such as an arc lamp with a liquid crystal display panel, applied to the entire surface of the vat of photopolymer resin in a single scan. This makes DLP faster than SLA.

Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM)

Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) is a 3D printing process that creates objects layer by layer using thermoplastic filaments. Starting with a CAD design, the filament is heated, extruded through a nozzle, and deposited in thin strands, cooling and solidifying to form each layer.

Vacuum Casting

Vacuum Casting, also known as Silicon Moulding, RTV, or Rapid Tooling, is ideal for producing high-quality, durable, and accurate parts for competitive market timelines. This process is perfect for R&D testing and short-run production.

CNC Machining

CNC Machining offers precision machining and sheet metal fabrication with advanced capabilities like laser cutting, plasma and waterjet cutting, CNC bending, and power coating. Our expertise spans materials such as stainless steel, aluminum, brass, and copper.
